Introduction

RpfB is a gene responsible for the creation of the protein Resuscitation-promoting factor in the Mycobacterium Tuberculosis (Mtb). Resuscitation of Mtb is crucial to the aetiology of Tuberculosis, because latent tuberculosis is estimated to affect one-third of the world population. The resuscitation-promoting factor RpfB is mainly responsible for Mtb resuscitation from dormancy. Given the impact of latent Tuberculosis, RpfB represents an interesting target for tuberculosis drug discovery. However, no molecular models of substrate binding and catalysis are hitherto available for this enzyme.
